before you buy more food, did you know...

Globally speaking, over 1/3 of the food produced gets lost or wasted each year.
Thats approximately 1.3 billon tons overall, with devloped countries wasting around 670 million tons. According to the Food and Agriculture Organization, more then 40% of that is lost at the consumer and retail level of our production chain. Extra produce and food production equates to almost a third of greenhouse gas emissions, a loss of over $680 per country per year, and over a couple thousand litres of water.

So, what can you do?

Be a more informed consumer:


  • - know where your food comes from
  • - plan ahead to how much you need
  • - calculate realistically what you will consume
  • - freeze, preserve, and can your leftovers
  • - be adventurous! As long as it is safe and edible, use it in recipes
